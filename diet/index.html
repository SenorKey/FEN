🍽 Nutrition

Eat at least 2,000–2,400 calories

Chronic undereating tanks testosterone, raises cortisol, and stalls recovery. Prioritize real food over filler.

Hit 130–160g of protein

Non-negotiable for muscle maintenance and repair at this body weight and activity level. Spread across your eating window if possible.

Eat 25–35g of fiber

Feeds beneficial gut bacteria, stabilizes blood sugar, and supports serotonin production. Vegetables, oats, beans, fruit.

Eat at least one serving of vegetables

Even a bag of microwaved broccoli or a handful of spinach in your eggs. Covers potassium, magnesium, and folate that multivitamins only partially replace.

Keep added sugar under 36g

AHA recommended limit for men. Reduces insulin spikes, visceral fat storage, anxiety-producing crashes, and gut dysbiosis.

Take multivitamin + omega-3 + 5g creatine with food

Fat-soluble vitamins absorb better alongside a meal. Mix creatine into your protein shake or water daily — including rest days. Consistency matters more than timing.

💧 Hydration

Drink at least 100–120oz of water

Creatine draws water into muscle cells, making consistent hydration essential — not optional. Dehydration mimics anxiety symptoms, impairs recovery, and can cause cramping. Spread throughout the day.

🏋 Exercise

4x/week — Gym session with progressive intensity

Increase weight or reps over time. Creatine supports heavier short-burst efforts, so lean into that — push for one more rep or a slightly heavier set. Start with a 1-mile jog warmup.

Daily — 20-minute walk, preferably in sunlight

Supports vitamin D, lowers cortisol, regulates circadian rhythm for better sleep. Morning sun is most effective.

🌙 Sleep

Get 7–9 hours of sleep

Sleep deprivation raises cortisol, promotes visceral fat storage, increases appetite for sugar, and directly triggers anxiety. This is foundational.

Stop eating 2–3 hours before bed

Reduces acid reflux, chest tightness when lying down, and improves sleep quality. With a 4–7pm window, aim to be done by 7pm.

Consistent sleep and wake time (±30 min)

Regulates cortisol rhythm. Erratic sleep schedules keep the body in a stress state even when total hours are adequate.

Limit screens 30–60 min before bed

Blue light suppresses melatonin. If unavoidable, use night mode or blue-light glasses.

🧠 Stress & Mental Health

10 minutes of intentional downtime

Stretching, deep breathing, sitting outside, or anything that isn't a screen. Actively lowers cortisol and breaks the anxiety feedback loop.

Notes

Consistency matters more than perfection. Hitting 80% of this daily will produce noticeable changes in energy, anxiety, and body composition within 3–6 weeks.

Creatine may add 3–5 lbs of water weight in the first two weeks. This is normal intramuscular water — not fat. Don't misread the scale.
